## What this page is for
Product video is stricter than general cinematic video. The product must stay readable, geometry should not drift, surfaces need clean reflections, and the camera should help the buyer understand the item. A beautiful clip is not useful if the product changes shape, the label melts, or the reveal hides the object.

## Best picks
1. **[LTX 2.3 Pro](/models/ltx-2-3-pro)** - best production workflow for product generation, extension, retakes, audio-led variants, and 4K-capable finishing.
2. **[Seedance 2.0](/models/seedance-2-0)** - best for product references, cinematic motion, and campaign variants.
3. **[Kling 3 Pro](/models/kling-3-pro)** - best for structured product reveals, multi-shot demos, Elements, and end-frame planning.
4. **[Happy Horse 1.0](/models/happy-horse-1-0)** - best when product videos need image inputs, R2V references, V2V edits, native audio, or lip-sync in one model.

## Why these models rank here
LTX 2.3 Pro is the strongest production workflow choice rather than a one-off prompt. It can generate, extend, retake, and move toward higher-resolution delivery. That is useful when a product shot is almost right but one section needs repair.

Seedance 2.0 is strong when the product video depends on references: approved product image, campaign look, audio direction, or style frame. It is a practical choice for creating multiple ad variants around the same product world.

Kling 3 Pro is the best choice for a product story: macro detail, use-case shot, hero reveal, final landing frame. Its multi-shot control and Elements workflow make it easier to keep the product or prop anchored across the sequence.

Happy Horse 1.0 remains useful when product video may move between modes. A team can start from a product image, add R2V references, generate native audio or lip-sync for a social cut, and use V2V editing when the first clip needs a visual change, but Seedance and Kling are stronger for image quality and motion.

## Product prompt checklist
- State what the product is and what must stay stable.
- Keep the product visible in the first or second beat.
- Use simple camera movement: push-in, orbit, rack focus, tabletop slide.
- Avoid asking for readable packaging text unless the source is already clean and controlled.
- Use a final landing frame for ecommerce or ad thumbnails.

## FAQ
### What is the best AI video generator for product videos?
LTX 2.3 Pro is the best production pick for product videos, with Seedance 2.0 close behind for reference-led campaign variants. Kling 3 Pro is strongest for structured product sequences, and Happy Horse 1.0 is useful when unified editing/audio/reference modes matter.

### Which model is best for ecommerce videos?
Use LTX 2.3 Pro for controlled production, Seedance 2.0 for campaign variants, and Kling 3 Pro for structured product reveals.

### How do I avoid product drift?
Use a reference image, keep one product action per shot, and state exactly what must remain unchanged.
